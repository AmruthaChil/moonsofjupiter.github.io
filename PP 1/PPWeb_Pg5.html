  1. Aitne

      2. Facts:
    1. This moon was discovered on 9 December 2001 by Scott S. Sheppard, David C. Jewitt and Jan T. Kleyna at the Mauna Kea observatory in Hawaii.
    2. It is 4247 times smaller than Earth.
    3. Its orbit distance is 23,315,000 km.
    4. It completes one orbit in about 730 Earth days.

      5. Numbers:
    5. It has an equatorial circumference of 9.4 km.
    6. It has a surface area of 28.27 km2.
    7. It has a surface gravity of 0.001 m/s2.

      8. Name:
    8. It was originally named S/2001 J11.
    9. Aitne is named after a Sicilian nymph who was raped by Jupiter when he was in the form of a vulture.
    10. This name is derived from the Greek for I Burn. It is commonly associated with Sicily’s volcano, Mount Etna.

      11. Habitable?
    11. This moon is irregularly shaped and also has a very small surface area and gravity. Thus, it is most likely not habitable.
