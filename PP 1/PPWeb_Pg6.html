Himalia Group

The moons in this group have a prograde orbit - which means that they orbit Jupiter in its direction of rotation. Thus, their names end with an "a" and not an "e" as in retrograde moons.

  1. Dia

      2. Facts:
    1. Dia was discovered in 2000 by S.S. Sheppard, D.C. Jewitt, Y. Fernandez and G. Magnier on Mount Kea in Hawaii.
    2. It is 3185.5 times smaller than Earth.
    3. It has a prograde orbit.
    4. Its orbital distance is 12,555,000 km.

      5. Numbers:
    5. Its equatorial circumference is 12.6 km.
    6. It has a surface area of 50.27 km 2.

      7. Name:
    7. “Dia” means “she who belongs to Zeus” in Greek.
    8. Dia was the daughter of the seashore.
    9. It was originally called S/2000 J11.

      10. Habitable?
    10. This moon is a very small one with no special features, thus, it is unlikely to be habitable.

  2. Elara

      3. Facts:
    1. Discovered on 5 January 1905 by Charles Dillon Perrine on Mount Hamilton
    2. It is the eighth largest moon of Jupiter. It is also the second biggest in the Himalia group.
    3. It is probably a part of an asteroid.
    4. It is 11,741,000 km away from Jupiter.
    5. It takes about 260 Earth days to complete an orbit.
    6. Elara is 148.2 times smaller than Earth.

      7. Numbers:
    7. Elara has an equatorial circumference of 43 km.
    8. Surface Area of this moon is 23,235.22 km2.
    9. It has a surface gravity of 0.031 m/s/2.

      10. Name:
    10. This moon is named for one of Zeus’ lovers.
    11. Zeus hid her from his wife Hera by placing her deep underneath Earth.
    12. Her son with Zeus was a giant called Tityas.

      13. Habitable?
    13. This moon doesn’t seem to be habitable either and has the common case of a very less surface area and surface gravity.

  3. Himalia

      4. Facts:
    1. Discovered on 3 December 1904 by Charles Dillion Perrine on Mount Hamilton at the University of California.
    2. It is 75 times smaller than Earth.
    3. It is the fifth-largest moon of Jupiter, which means that it is the largest after the Galilean moons.
    4. It is the largest moon of the Himalia group.
    5. It may be the largest chunk of the asteroid that formed all the other moons in this group.
    6. It has an orbit distance of 11,461,000 km.
    7. Himalia takes about 251 Earth days to revolve once around Jupiter.

      8. Numbers:
    8. It has an equatorial radius on 534.1 km.
    9. Himalia has a surface area of 90,792 km2.
    10. It’s surface gravity is 0.062 m/s2.

      11. Name:
    11. It is named after a nymph of the island of Rhodes, who was another lover of Zeus.
    12. She has three sons: Spartacus, Cronos and Cytus.

      13. Habitable?
    13. This moon has quite a large surface area, but its surface gravity is close to 160 times lesser than that of Earth. This is a factor that makes it non- habitable.

  4. Leda

      5. Facts:
    1. This moon was discovered on 14 September 1974 by Charles Thomas Kowal at Mount Palomar.
    2. It is a part of an asteroid that formed the entire Himalia group.
    3. Its orbit distance is 11,165,000 km.
    4. This moon takes about 241 Earth days to complete one orbit around Jupiter.

      5. Numbers:
    5. This moon has an equatorial circumference of 62.8 km.
    6. This moon has a surface area of 1,256.64 km2.
    7. Its surface gravity is 0.007 m/s2.

      8. Name:
    8. There are many legends around the greek mythological character after whom this moon is named.
    9. One of them is that Leda was seduced by Zeus, who had taken the form of a swan.
    10. She has 2 sons - Pollux and Helen.

      11. Habitable?
    11. This moon also has very less surface area and gravity. There is also no guarantee of a good atmosphere or water. Thus, this moon is also not habitable.

  5. Lysithea

      6. Facts:
    1. Discovered on 6 July 1938, by Seth Barnes Nicholson at the Mount Wilson Observatory.
    2. It is the second smallest moon in the Himalia Group.
    3. This is also probably a piece of an asteroid, just like all the others in the Himalia group.
    4. It is 353.9 times smaller than Earth.
    5. Its orbital distance from Jupiter is 11,717,000 km.
    6. It takes about 259 Earth days to complete one orbit.

      7. Numbers:
    7. This moon has an equatorial circumference of 113.1 km.
    8. The surface area is 4,071.50 km2
    9. It has a surface gravity of 0.013 m/s2

      10. Name:
    10. She is named after another lover of Zeus (or Jupiter).
    11. When asked for a gift, she was tricked by Hera, Zeus’ wife, to ask Zeus to come to her the same way he wooed the latter. Zeus cam riding on his chariot, and launched a thunderbolt into the room.
    12. While Lysithea died out of fear, Zeus stitched the unborn Dionysus to his thigh and undid the stitches when he was ready.

      13. Habitable?
    13. This moon also has an extremely small surface gravity and area. It is also likely to be irregularly shaped. Thus, it isn’t habitable.

  6. Ersa

      7. Facts:
    1. This small moon was discovered in 2017.
    2. It was officially designated the name - S/2018 J1.

      3. Name:
    3. Ersa is the sister of Pandia in Greek mythology.
    4. She is the daughter of Zeus and Selene (the moon goddess).
    5. Ersa is the goddess of Dew.

      6. Habitable?
    6. This is a recently discovered moon and not much is known about it. Thus, it is unsure of whether this moon is habitable or not.

  7. Pandia

      8. Facts:
    1. This small moon was discovered in 2017.
    2. It was officially designated the name - S/2017 J4.

      3. Name:
    3. Pandia is the sister of Ersa in Greek mythology.
    4. She is the daughter of Zeus and Selene (the moon goddess).
    5. Pandia is the goddess of the full moon.

      6. Habitable?
    6. This is a recently discovered moon and not much is known about it, like Ersa. Thus, it is unsure of whether this moon is habitable or not.
