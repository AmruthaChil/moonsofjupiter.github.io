Anake Group

The moons in this group are parts of asteroids.

  1. Anake

      2. Facts:
    1. The largest member of the Ananke group.
    2. It was discovered on 28 September 1951 by Seth Barnes Nicholson, at the Mount Wilson Observatory in California.
    3. It might have been an asteroid, which Jupiter’s gravity had captured.
    4. Its most noticed colours are grey and light red.
    5. Ananke is 455.1 times smaller than Earth.
    6. It takes about 630 Earth days to complete one orbit.
    7. Its mean distance from Jupiter (orbital distance) is 21,276,000 km.

      8. Numbers:
    8. Its equatorial circumference is 88 km.
    9. It has a surface area of 2,463.01 km2.
    10. It has a surface gravity of 0.010 m/s2.

      11. Name:
    11. This moon is named after the Greek goddess Ananke.
    12. Ananke was the embodiment of fate and necessity in ancient Greek literature and is a Primordial deity (existing before the start of time).

      13. Habitable?
    13. This moon has a very small surface area and surface gravity, compared to Earth. Thus, it is also not habitable.
