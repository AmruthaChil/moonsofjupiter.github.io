Pasiphae Group

The moons in this group are parts of asteroids.

  1. Aoede

      2. Facts:
    1. This moon was discovered on 8 February 2003 by Scott S. Sheppard, David C. Jewitt, Jan T. Kleyna, Yanga R. Fernandez, and Henry H. Hsieh at the Mauna Kea Observatory in Hawaii.
    2. It is 3185 times smaller than Earth.
    3. Its orbit distance is 23,969,000 km.
    4. It completes one orbit in about 761 Earth days.

      5. Numbers:
    5. It has an equatorial circumference of 12.6 km.
    6. It has a surface area of 50.27 km2.
    7. It has a surface gravity of 0.002 m/s2.

      8. Name:
    8. It was originally called S/2003 J7.
    9. Aoede is named after one of the Muses, who were the daughters of Zeus/Jupiter.
    10. Aoede means “song”.

      11. Habitable?
    11. This moon is irregularly shaped and also has a very small surface area and gravity. Thus, it is most likely not habitable.

  3. Callirrhoe

      4. Facts:
    1. This moon was discovered on 19 October 1999 by Jim V. Scotti, Timothy B. Spahr, Robert S. McMillan, Jeffrey A. Larsen, Joe Montani, Arianna E. Gleason, and Tom Gehrels at Kitt Peak.
    2. This moon was originally thought of being an asteroid, but after a series of calculations about its orbit, it was published as a satellite of Jupiter.
    3. It is 1481 times smaller than Earth.
    4. Its orbit distance is 24,102,000 km.
    5. It completes one orbit in about 759 Earth days.

      6. Numbers:
    6. It has an equatorial circumference of 27 km.
    7. It has a surface area of 232.35 km2.
    8. It has a surface gravity of 0.003 m/s2.

      9. Name:
    9. It was originally called S/1999 J1.
    10. This moon is named after ‘Callirrhoe’, the daughter of the river god Achelous.

      11. Habitable?
    11. This moon has a surface area that is larger than most moons of Jupiter. It also has a higher surface gravity than other moons but is still very much smaller than that of Earth’s. Thus, it is not sure whether it is habitable or not.

  9. Pasiphae

      10. Facts:
    1. This moon was discovered on 27 January 1908 by Philibert Jacques Melotte at the Greenwich Observatory.
    2. It is 212 times smaller than Earth.
    3. This is the largest moon in this moon group.
    4. Its orbit distance is 23,624,000km.
    5. It completes one orbit in about 744 Earth days.

      6. Numbers:
    6. It has an equatorial circumference of 188.5 km.
    7. It has a surface area of 11,309 km2.
    8. It has a surface gravity of 0.022 m/s2.

      9. Name:
    9. This moon is named after the wife of Minos, the ruler of Crete.
    10. She is said to be the mother of the Minotaur, the creature that has the body of a human and the head of a bull.

      11. Habitable?
    11. This moon has the highest surface area amongst most of Jupiter’s moons (excluding the Galilean moons). Based on the elements present on its surface and atmosphere, it can be decided whether it’s habitable or not.
