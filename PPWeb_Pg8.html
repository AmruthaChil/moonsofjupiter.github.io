Ungrouped and Unnamed moons

The moons in this group are either ungrouped or are not confirmed to be Jupiter's moons yet.


Ungrouped moons:


Carpo
    Facts:
  1. This moon was discovered on 26 February 2003, by Scott S. Sheppard and his team at the Mauna Kea Observatory.
  2. This moon has a prograde orbit around Jupiter, meaning it travels in the same direction on its orbit as the direction of rotation of Jupiter.
  3. This is the farthest known moon of Jupiter to have this feature.
  4. This moon is 4247 times smaller than Earth.
  5. It has an orbit distance of 17,078,000 km.

    6. Numbers:
  6. This moon has an equatorial circumference of 9.4 km.
  7. It has a volume of 14 km3.
  8. Its mass is 44X1012 kg.
  9. It has a surface area of 28.27 km2.
  10. It has a surface gravity of 0.001 m/s2

    11. Name:
  11. This moon was originally called S/2003 J20.
  12. Carpo was named after one of the three Athenian Goddesses.
  13. Carpo is the goddess of flowers of spring and autumn.

    14. Habitable?
  14. This moon also has a very small surface gravity and area. Also, not much information is available about this moon, except for some superficial information about it. Thus, it is also not likely to be habitable.

Themisto
    Facts:
  1. This moon was discovered on 30 September 1977, by Charles Thomas Kowal and Elizabeth Roemer.
  2. It was lost till 2000 when it was found again by Scott S. Sheppard, David C. Jewitt, Yanga Roland Fernandez and Eugene A. Magnier.
  3. It has a Prograde orbit, i.e, it travels in the same direction in which Jupiter rotates.
  4. It is 1593 times smaller than Earth.
  5. Its orbit distance is 7,507,000 km.
  6. It completes one orbit in 130 Earth days.

    7. Numbers:
  7. It has an equatorial circumference of 25.1 km.
  8. It has a volume of 268 km3.
  9. Its mass is 7.0X1012 kg.
  10. It has a surface area of 201.06 km2.
  11. It has a surface gravity of 0.003 m/s2.

    12. Name:
  12. It was first named S/1975 J1 and renamed S/2000 J1 after being rediscovered.
  13. Themisto is the mother of Ister by Zeus.
  14. She was changed into a bear by Zeus’ wife and sister Hera, who was jealous of the former.
  15. Themisto is the first among all of Jupiter’s satellites to be given a name ending in “o”.

    16. Habitable?
  16. This moon also doesn’t seem to have any special features that may allow for life on it.

Valetudo
    Facts:
  1. This moon was first seen in 2017.
  2. The discovery announcement was made in July 2018.

    3. Name:
  3. Its official name is S/2016 J2.
  4. It is named after Valetudo, health and hygiene goddess.

    5. Habitable?
  5. Limited information is available about this moon, and hence, it is difficult to decide whether it is habitable.
